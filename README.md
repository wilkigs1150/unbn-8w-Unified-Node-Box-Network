# UNBN-8W: Unified Node Box Network

**A framework for mapping the hidden geometry, structural tension, and breaking points of complex systems.**

## What is the UNBN-8W?
The UNBN-8W (Unified Node Box Network) is an analytical framework and prompt engineering architecture designed to be used alongside Large Language Models (LLMs). 

Rather than treating data as static numbers or historical timelines, the UNBN-8W forces an AI to analyze information as a physical topology. It maps complex datasets—ranging from geopolitical conflicts and macroeconomic markets to corporate ecosystems—onto an **8-Node Coherence Lattice**. 

By treating a system as a "Dynamic Oscillator" that must constantly rebalance internal tension against external pressure, this framework allows you to identify exactly where a system is bottlenecking, how it is drifting, and precisely what will trigger its structural collapse (the **Coherency Inversion**).

---

## The Master Prompt
You do not need to code a physics engine to use this framework; you simply use the LLM as your compiler. Feed your target dataset (articles, market reports, historical summaries) into any advanced AI, followed by this exact Master Question:

> **"Using the data provided, generate an Individual Coherency Well Status Report and Coherence Map for this system, including current well structure, stability, drift, transport tension, and projected evolution across relevant timeframes."**

---

## How to Read the Output
When the AI applies the UNBN-8W ruleset, it will return a Coherency Well Report broken down into five core metrics:

### 1. Current Well Structure
Identifies the "anchors" or primary rendering engines of the system. What are the opposing forces or core nodes, and where is their exact Interference Point?
### 2. Stability
Measures the friction holding the lattice together. True stability is rare; most systems maintain baseline equilibrium through highly pressurized, localized workarounds or resource drains.
### 3. Drift
Tracks the directional vector. Is the system maintaining its current state, or is the physical/psychological hardware actively mutating toward a new reality?
### 4. Transport Tension
Identifies the bottlenecks. This is where the energy required to maintain the system violently mismatches the incoming resources. Tension points are the "sparks" that precede systemic failure.
### 5. Projected Evolution & The Inversion Horizon
Maps the breaking point. A **Coherency Inversion** occurs when a system can no longer process pressure through its current structure and is forced to radically flip its baseline state to survive. This metric projects the exact structural pillar that must break to trigger that inversion.

## Use Cases
The UNBN-8W is entirely discipline-agnostic. Because it maps the *mechanics of flow and tension* rather than specific subject matter, it can be applied to:
* **Macroeconomics:** Tracking consumer debt bottlenecks against inflation structures.
* **Geopolitics:** Mapping military attrition and domestic political friction.
* **Corporate Architecture:** Identifying localized tensions between supply chains and C-suite directives.
* **Behavioral Data:** Analyzing the breaking points of social movements or market panic.
