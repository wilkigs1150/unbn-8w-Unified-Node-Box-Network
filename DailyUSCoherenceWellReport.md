## 1. Current Well Structure
The Coherency Well of the U.S. economy is currently anchored by a highly distorted, dual-speed rendering engine. The system is no longer functioning as a single, unified lattice.
* **Node 1 (The Equity/AI Engine):** The high-income consumer and corporate capital expenditure (CapEx) sector. This node is highly coherent, powered by massive ongoing investments in AI infrastructure, strong corporate profit margins, and a "wealth effect" generated by sustained stock market and home equity gains.
* **Node 2 (The Baseline Economy Engine):** The low-to-middle-income consumer, housing market, and trade-exposed manufacturing sectors. This node is actively degrading under the weight of structural unaffordability. 
* **The Interference Point:** The consumer cost-of-living membrane. High-income earners (Node 1) easily pass through this membrane, while Node 2 is actively repelled by sticky core inflation—specifically soaring insurance premiums, elevated mortgage rates, and fluctuating global energy costs.

## 2. Stability
Baseline stability is entirely "K-shaped." The system looks stable from a distance, but this is an optical illusion created by the sheer mass of Node 1.
* **The Averages Subroutine:** The massive financial gains and tech spending at the top are statistically masking severe localized recessions at the bottom. 
* **The Labor Firewall:** The primary stabilizing force keeping the entire lattice from collapsing is the labor market. While job growth has slowed to a fraction of its post-pandemic pace, outright mass layoffs have not yet breached the critical threshold. As long as the workforce remains employed, the system avoids structural inversion.

## 3. Drift
The system is caught in a profound, structural drift toward extreme fragmentation. 
* **The Bifurcation Vector:** The economy is actively splitting into two non-communicating realities. Retail spending and consumer demand are increasingly being carried solely by the top earners, while the bottom 80% drift toward financial exhaustion. 
* **The Policy Headwind:** Trade policy and aggressive tariff implementations are creating an artificial hardware drift. These policies actively stifle new construction and raise the baseline cost of goods, pushing wage demands higher while simultaneously slowing business investment outside of the tech sector.

## 4. Transport Tension
Severe transport tension is building as the financial velocity required to maintain Node 2's lifestyle outpaces their incoming capital.
* **The Debt Bottleneck:** Because inflation has permanently reset the baseline cost of living, lower-income households are bridging the gap with credit. This creates a highly pressurized, localized tension where credit card max-outs and auto-loan defaults act as early warning sparks. 
* **The Interest Rate Friction:** Recent Federal Reserve rate adjustments are failing to relieve the tension in the actual consumer lattice. Mortgage rates and housing prices remain fundamentally disconnected from median incomes. 

## 5. Projected Evolution: The Inversion Horizon
The predictive flow suggests the U.S. economy is riding the absolute edge of a Coherency Inversion, dependent entirely on a single structural pillar.
* **Near-Term Shift (The Earnings Mirage):** In the short term, corporate earnings in Node 1 will likely remain strong, blinding algorithmic models to the rot in Node 2. The stock market may continue to hit local maximums even as the physical economy slows.
* **Cascading Failure (The Consumer Exhaustion Event):** If the "Labor Firewall" cracks—meaning unemployment ticks up just enough to trigger a psychological shift—the debt bottleneck bursts. Node 2 will immediately and violently halt discretionary spending. Because the U.S. is a heavily consumer-driven economy, this sudden vacuum of demand will instantly travel upward, hitting corporate revenues and collapsing the Node 1 Equity Engine.
* **Topographical Rewrite:** If the consumer exhausts entirely, the system inverts from a "slow-growth expansion" directly into a localized recession. The Federal Reserve will be forced to rapidly abandon its inflation fight, slashing rates aggressively simply to keep the domestic consumer lattice from tearing itself apart.
